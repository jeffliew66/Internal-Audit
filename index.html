Band definitions

Low (4–8)
Routine monitoring; may rotate beyond 36 months if residual stays green.
Medium (9–14)
Standard cycle within rolling plan; thematic review every 24 months.
High (15–19)
Near-mandatory annual coverage; escalation if control degradation detected.
Critical (20–25)
Board-visible inherent exposure; continuous assurance hook or dedicated programme.

Control risk (design & operating)

Engagement proves · Master defines

Control risk reflects how much reliance can be placed on controls to mitigate inherent exposure. Master defines how testers score design vs. operating effectiveness and how deficiencies roll up.

Rating Design Operating Master interpretation
Effective Fit-for-purpose, addresses risk Consistently applied Supports substantive reduction / reliance per policy matrix
Needs improvement Gaps in precision / coverage Exceptions within tolerance Residual capped one band above inherent-minus-controls estimate
Ineffective Missing / poorly designed Material failures observed Treat as failed mitigation — escalate residual to inherent corridor unless compensating controls evidenced

Residual risk computation

Illustrative master formula — implement your actuarial / qualitative blend in policy.

// After controls considered
residual_score = clamp(
  inherent_score × control_factor × detection_factor,
  min_band, max_band
);

// control_factor: 1.0 ineffective → 0.35 highly effective
// detection_factor: supervisory / analytics uplift

Findings reference residual band movement (worse / unchanged / improved) where possible, not only narrative severity.

Residual outcome bands

Band Score range Reporting posture

Elevated 6–10 Management action plan · IA validates closures
Above appetite 11–16 Audit committee visibility · timeline commitments
Critical exposure 17+ Executive response · possible restriction / redesign

Type Audience Risk positioning

Standard sections

  1. Scope & objectives — universe references, criteria, exclusions.
  2. Risk profile — inherent highlights; materiality footnotes reserved for company layer.
  3. Approach — methodology pack IDs used.
  4. Observations — findings grouped by theme & residual impact.
  5. Management response — actions with dates & owners.
  6. Opinion / conclusion — master-controlled wording library.
